Veg Fried Rice

Prep Time

15 minutes

Cook Time

20 minutes

Total Time

35 minutes

Servings

4 people

Ingredients

    Hyderabadi Veg Biryani Ingredients For Cooking Rice:>
    • 1 cup basmati rice (190 to 200 grams)
    • 4 to 4.5 cups water
    • ½ teaspoon salt
    • 2 to 3 drops toasted sesame oil or any cooking oil (optional)
    For Preparing Vegetables:>
    • ¼ cup finely chopped spring white onions (scallions)
    • ¼ cup finely chopped carrots
    • ¼ cup finely chopped french beans
    • ¼ cup finely chopped capsicum (bell peppers, any color)
    • 1 cup chopped button mushrooms
    • 1 tablespoon finely chopped celery
    • ¼ to ½ cup chopped cabbage (optional)
    For Stir-Frying:>
    • 3 tablespoons cooking oil (neutral taste)
    • 1 whole star anise (optional)
    • 1 teaspoon finely chopped garlic
    • ½ teaspoon finely chopped ginger
    For Seasoning:
    • 1 tablespoon soy sauce (adjust to taste)
    • ½ teaspoon ground black pepper
    • Salt (to taste, keeping in mind the soy sauce will add some saltiness)
    Optional:
    • 1 tablespoon rice wine or rice vinegar (or other mild vinegar)
    • Additional chopped spring onions for garnish

Steps to Make Recipe

Prep and Cook Rice
  1. A great vegetable fried rice recipe begins with well-cooked white rice. To do so, first, soak 1 cup of basmati rice (190 to 200 grams) in water for 30 minutes. Then drain and set the rice aside.
    Tip: You can opt for a good quality aged long-grained rice if you do not have Basmati rice. Jasmine rice will also work in a pinch.
  2. In a large pot, add 4 to 4.5 cups of water, ½ teaspoon salt, and 2 to 3 drops of toasted sesame oil (or any cooking oil), and set over high heat on the stovetop.
  3. Let the water reach a full boil.
  4. Add the soaked and drained basmati rice.
  5. Simmer the rice uncovered on medium to medium-high heat.
  6. Cook rice until al dente, or just about cooked. It’s important that you do not overcook the rice during this step in order to make the best Chinese fried rice dish. Overcooking rice or even cooking it completely will break the rice grains while stir-frying.
  7. Quickly strain the rice in a colander, and let it cool completely at room temperature. Refrigerating the rice for 30 minutes also helps. Simply cover cooked rice and refrigerate.
  8. You can also rinse the rice with water so that the rice stops cooking. Use your hands to gently stir the cooked rice grains around while rinsing to ensure that every grain is cooled. Then drain thoroughly.
  9. Cover cooked rice and set aside to cool.
Prep Vegetables
  1. While rice is cooling, finely chop the veggies. Because french beans tend to be tougher than the other mixed vegetables, I recommend chopping them very finely. You also have the option to blanch them first before adding, or you can begin cooking them in this recipe before the rest of the vegetables are added.
    For my favorite version of fried rice recipe, you’ll need ¼ cup of each of the following ingredients, finely chopped:
    • Spring white onions (scallions) (not in the image below)
    • Carrots
    • French beans
    • Capsicum (bell peppers, your choice of color – green, red, or yellow)
    • 1 cup chopped button mushrooms
    • 1 tablespoon finely chopped celery
    • ¼ to ½ cup chopped cabbage (optional)
Stir Fry Veggies
  1. Now it’s time to stir-fry the vegetables. Start by heating 3 tablespoons of preferred cooking oil in a wok or a deep skillet. Add 1 whole star anise and fry for a few seconds until the oil becomes fragrant.
    Tip: Use a good, trusted, heavy pan, skillet, or wok for stir-frying so that the tender fresh veggies do not get stuck or burn when frying at medium to high heat. Use oil with a neutral taste and flavor.
  2. If you do not have star anise, simply skip adding it and move to the next step.
  3. Add 1 teaspoon of finely chopped garlic and ½ teaspoon finely chopped ginger. Cook for only a moment as you don’t want to brown or burn the garlic.
  4. Add the chopped spring onions, and sauté for a minute or two on medium-low to medium heat.
  5. Then add the finely chopped french beans – or, add these before the onions if you want a softer bean texture.
  6. Continue to stir-fry both the onions and french beans for 2 to 3 minutes over medium to medium-high heat.
  7. Now add the remaining finely chopped veggies, including mushrooms and celery. Increase the heat to high to thoroughly cook all of the ingredients.
  8. Continuously toss and stir while frying so that the veggies are uniformly cooked and do not get burnt – for about 4 to 6 minutes. You want the vegetables to be cooked and fried but still retain some great crunch.
Add Seasonings
  1. Once the veggies have cooked, add 1 tablespoon of soy sauce. Stir and mix well.
    Tip: You can add less (1 to 2 teaspoons) or more (about 2 to 3 tablespoons) soy sauce if you prefer. I recommend going for naturally brewed soy sauce or tamari.
    Note: If you do not like or prefer soy sauce, then omit it adding to the dish.
  2. Taste the dish, and then add ½ teaspoon of ground black pepper and as much salt as you like. The Chinese fried rice will already be slightly salty because of the soy sauce, so not much additional salt should be needed.
  3. Mix well to combine the flavors.
Make Veg Fried Rice>
  1. You are now ready to put everything together to make the veg fried rice. To the wok or skillet of veggies, add the cooked and cooled rice – 1 cup or so at a time.
  2. Reduce the heat to medium or medium-high. Use a spoon or spatula to gently fold the cooked rice into the mixed vegetables.
  3. With the heat still on medium to medium-high, continue to gently mix and fry the vegetables with rice for a couple of minutes.
  4. You now have the option to stir in 1 tablespoon of rice wine or rice vinegar (or other mild vinegar), if you like. This adds a terrific note of sourness to the fried rice that isn’t necessary but is quite lovely.
  5. Lastly, add more fresh chopped spring onions. You can either sprinkle on top as a garnish or mix into the fried rice to incorporate.
  6. Serve Veg Fried Rice hot by itself for a light yet hearty meal, or enjoy it as a side dish along with your other favorite Chinese recipes.

Recipe Tips

• Rice Preparation: Soak basmati rice for 30 minutes before cooking, then drain and cool it completely. Refrigeration helps in making the rice firmer and less sticky, which is ideal for stir-frying. • Vegetables: Chop vegetables finely to ensure they cook quickly and evenly. You can also blanch tougher vegetables like french beans before adding them to the stir-fry. • Stir-Frying: Use a heavy pan, skillet, or wok to prevent burning and ensure even cooking. Stir-fry vegetables on high heat for the best texture. • Seasoning: Adjust soy sauce and other seasonings to taste, especially if you prefer a lighter or stronger flavor.
