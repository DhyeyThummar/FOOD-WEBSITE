Veg Pulao

Prep Time

15 minutes

Cook Time

20 minutes

Total Time

35 minutes

Servings

4 people

Ingredients

    Hyderabadi Veg Biryani Ingredients For Soaking Rice:
    • 1.5 cups basmati rice
    For Prep Vegetables:
    • 1 large onion (thinly sliced)
    • 1 to 1.5 cups mixed chopped vegetables (e.g., carrots, beans, peas, cauliflower)
    • 1 to 1.5 inches fresh ginger
    • 4 to 5 small to medium garlic cloves (peeled)
    • 1 to 2 green chilies (green peppers)
    • ½ cup chopped tomatoes
    • 2 to 3 tablespoons chopped cilantro (coriander leaves)
    • 2 tablespoons chopped mint leaves (optional)
    Whole Spices (Use as desired, skipping some if preferred):
    • 1 to 2 bay leaves (tej patta)
    • 3 to 4 green cardamoms
    • 3 to 4 cloves
    • 1 to 2 inch cinnamon stick
    • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds (optional)
    • 1 to 2 black cardamoms (optional)
    • 2 to 3 black peppercorns (optional)
    • 1 star anise (optional)
    • 1 to 2 single strands of mace (optional)
    For Cooking Pulao:
    • 3 tablespoons ghee or oil
    • 2.5 to 3 cups water (or vegetable stock)
    • ¼ teaspoon lemon juice
    • Salt (to taste)
    Optional Garnishes:
    • Fried onions
    • Fried cashews
    • Fried raisins

Steps to Make Recipe

    1. Soak Rice:
      1. Begin by rinsing 1.5 cups basmati rice in water until it runs clear of starch. Next, soak the rice in enough water for 20 to 30 minutes.
      2. After 20 to 30 minutes, strain the rice of all the water and keep aside.
    2. Prep Vegetables:
      1. While the rice is soaking, rinse and chop the vegetables into small cubes. For cauliflower, chop into small to medium-sized florets. Peel, rinse, and thinly slice 1 large onion.
      2. Keep all the whole spices aside. You can skip black peppercorns, black cardamom, star anise, and mace if preferred.
    3. Prepare Ginger-Garlic-Chili Paste:
      1. Add chopped ginger (1 to 1.5 inches), garlic (4 to 5 small to medium garlic cloves, peeled), and 1 to 2 green chilies (green peppers) to a mortar and pestle.
      2. Crush to a paste. Alternatively, use a small grinder for crushing. Add a bit of water if needed when using a grinder or mixer.
    4. Fry Spices & Sauté Onions:
      1. Heat 3 tablespoons ghee or oil in a deep, thick-bottomed pot or pan.
      2. Once the ghee has melted and is hot, add all whole spices and fry for a few seconds until fragrant. The spices will splutter. Avoid letting the ghee smoke.
      3. Add 1 cup of thinly sliced onions to the pot.
      4. Mix the onions with the ghee and begin to sauté on low to medium-low heat. Stir frequently.
      5. Sauté onions until they become golden brown.
    5. Sauté Tomatoes, Veggies, and Herbs:
      1. Once the onions are golden, add the prepared ginger-garlic-chili paste.
      2. Stir and sauté until the raw aroma of ginger and garlic disappears.
      3. Add ½ cup chopped tomatoes and sauté for 2 to 3 minutes on low heat.
      4. Add 1 to 1.5 cups of mixed chopped vegetables.
      5. Add 2 to 3 tablespoons of chopped coriander leaves (cilantro) and 2 tablespoons of chopped mint leaves (optional).
      6. Mix and sauté again for 2 to 3 minutes on low heat.
    6. Add Rice:
      1. Add the soaked rice to the pot.
      2. Mix the rice gently with the rest of the ingredients.
      3. Sauté the rice gently for 1 to 2 minutes on low to medium-low heat, ensuring the rice is well coated with the oil or ghee.
    7. Cook Pulao:
      1. Pour 2.5 to 3 cups of water into the pot. Adjust the water quantity based on the quality of rice. You can also use vegetable stock instead of water.
      2. Add ¼ teaspoon lemon juice.
      3. Season with salt to taste. Mix well. Taste the water to ensure it is slightly salty. If not, add more salt.
      4. Stir and tightly cover the pot with its lid.
      5. Cook on low to medium heat until all the water is absorbed. Start with medium heat and lower it halfway through. Check occasionally to ensure there is enough water and stir gently with a fork if needed.
      6. Once the rice grains are cooked, fluff them with a fork and let the rice stand covered for 5 minutes.
      7. Serve the Veg Pulao garnished with coriander leaves (cilantro), mint leaves, or optional fried onions, cashews, and raisins.

Recipe Tips

You can adjust the quantity of water based on the type and quality of rice used. If using a small grinder or mixer, add a bit of water to make a paste of ginger, garlic, and green chilies. For best results, use a deep, thick-bottomed pot or pan to ensure even cooking.
