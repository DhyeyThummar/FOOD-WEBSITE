Vada Pav

Prep Time

15 minutes

Cook Time

20 minutes

Total Time

35 minutes

Servings

4 people

Ingredients

    Vada Pav Recipe
    • 2 large potatoes (about 350 grams)
    • 6 to 7 small/medium garlic cloves
    • 1 to 2 green chilies
    • 2 to 3 teaspoons oil (vegetable or any neutral oil)
    • ½ teaspoon mustard seeds
    • 7 to 8 curry leaves
    • A pinch of asafoetida (hing)
    • ⅛ teaspoon turmeric powder (about 2 to 3 pinches)
    • 1 to 2 tablespoons chopped coriander leaves (cilantro)
    • Salt, to taste
    • 1 to 1.25 cups besan (gram flour)
    • ⅛ teaspoon turmeric powder (about 2 to 3 pinches)
    • A pinch of asafoetida (hing)
    • A pinch of baking soda (optional)
    • Salt, to taste
    • ½ cup water (or as needed)
    • Oil, for deep frying (vegetable or any neutral oil)
    • Pav (Indian bread rolls)
    • Green chutney (recipe provided separately)
    • Sweet chutney (recipe provided separately)
    • Dry garlic chutney (optional; recipe provided separately)
    • Potato Stuffing: Ensure that the mashed potatoes are well-drained and cool before mixing with the tempering.
    • Batter Consistency: The batter should be medium-thick; adjust with water or gram flour as needed.
    • Frying: Heat oil to the appropriate temperature to ensure that the vadas are crispy and golden. Avoid overcrowding the pan.
    • Chutneys: Green and sweet chutneys can be made in advance and refrigerated.

Steps to Make Recipe

  1. Boil 2 large potatoes that weigh about 350 grams. Peel and then mash them with a fork in a bowl. You can boil or steam the potatoes in a pan, Instant Pot, or stovetop pressure cooker, adding water as needed. Remember to drain the water very well from the boiled potatoes before you mash them. Set aside the potatoes to cool. When they become warm, peel and mash them with a fork or a potato masher. Also, crush 6 to 7 small/medium-sized garlic cloves and 1 to 2 green chilies in a mortar and pestle.
  2. Heat 2 to 3 teaspoons of oil in a small pan. Add ½ teaspoon of mustard seeds and let them crackle. Then add 7 to 8 curry leaves and a pinch of asafoetida (hing). Stir and sauté for about 5 seconds on low heat.
  3. Add the crushed garlic cloves and green chilies mixture to the pan. Add ⅛ teaspoon of turmeric powder (about 2 to 3 pinches).
  4. Stir on low heat for a few seconds or until the raw aroma of garlic goes away.
  5. Pour this tempering mixture, along with the oil, into the mashed potatoes.
  6. Add 1 to 2 tablespoons of chopped coriander leaves (cilantro) and salt to taste.
  7. Mix everything well with a spoon. Taste and adjust salt if needed. Form small to medium balls from the mashed potato mixture and flatten them slightly. Cover and set aside.
  1. In another bowl, prepare a smooth batter using 1 to 1.25 cups besan (gram flour), ⅛ teaspoon turmeric powder (about 2 to 3 pinches), a pinch of asafoetida (hing), a pinch of baking soda (optional), salt, and ½ cup water or as needed. The batter should have a medium-thick, flowing consistency. Adjust with water or gram flour as needed. If the batter becomes too thin, add 1 or 2 tablespoons of gram flour. If it becomes too thick, add 1 or 2 tablespoons of water.
  1. Before dipping the batata vada in the batter, heat oil in a pan or wok (kadai). When the oil becomes hot, dip the batata vada in the batter to coat it evenly, then carefully and gently drop these batter-coated potato balls into the medium-hot oil. Note: Read more details on frying temperature in the ‘expert tips’ section below.
  2. Depending on the size of the pan or wok, you can fry more or fewer vadas at a time, but do not overcrowd the pan.
  3. When one side firms up and becomes slightly crisp and golden, turn the vadas gently with a slotted spoon.
  4. Fry the second side until golden and crisp, turning occasionally as needed. Place them on kitchen paper towels to absorb extra oil. Fry all the batata vadas in batches and set aside. Regulate the stovetop heat as needed when frying.
  1. Serve the vadas when they are still hot or warm. Arrange the chutneys and pav on your work surface. Slice the pav without breaking it into two parts and set aside. If you don’t have sweet chutney, you can make the vada pav with green chutney only. The recipes for green chutney and sweet chutney are provided in the recipe card below. You can prepare these chutneys ahead of time and refrigerate them.
  2. Spread both green chutney and sweet chutney on the sliced pav. Alternatively, spread green chutney on one side and sweet chutney on the other side.
  3. Sprinkle dry garlic chutney on the pav. This step is optional and can be skipped if you don’t have garlic chutney.
  4. Place the hot batata vada in the bread slices.
  5. Serve vada pav immediately to avoid sogginess. You can also serve some salted fried green chilies and both chutneys with it. This makes for a satisfying evening snack high in carbs, best enjoyed with a cup of hot tea or coffee.

Recipe Tips

You can adjust the quantity of water based on the type and quality of rice used. If using a small grinder or mixer, add a bit of water to make a paste of ginger, garlic, and green chilies. For best results, use a deep, thick-bottomed pot or pan to ensure even cooking.
