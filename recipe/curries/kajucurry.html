Kaju Curry

Prep Time

15 minutes

Cook Time

20 minutes

Total Time

35 minutes

Servings

4 people

Ingredients

    Kaju Curry
    • 1 tablespoon butter or oil
    • 1 cup whole cashews (120 grams)
    • 1 tej patta (Indian bay leaf)
    • 1.5 cups roughly chopped tomatoes (about 4 medium or 3 large tomatoes)
    • ½ cup water
    • 18 to 20 cashews
    • Tomatoes cooked in the previous step (after removing the tej patta)
    • 2 tablespoons butter
    • 1 teaspoon ginger-garlic paste (made from 1 inch ginger and 3 to 4 garlic cloves, crushed)
    • Cashew powder (from the earlier step)
    • ½ teaspoon Kashmiri red chili powder (or deghi mirch)
    • ⅔ to ¾ cup water (adjust as needed)
    • 1 to 2 slit green chilies
    • Salt (to taste)
    • ¼ to ½ teaspoon sugar (adjust to taste)
    • ½ teaspoon garam masala powder
    • 2 to 3 tablespoons low-fat cream or light cream or cooking cream, or
      • 1 to 2 tablespoons whipping cream
    • 1 teaspoon kasuri methi (dried fenugreek leaves), crushed

Steps to Make Recipe

  1. Chopped coriander leaves (for garnish)
  1. First heat 1 tablespoon butter or oil in a pan or kadai. Make sure that the butter does not brown. Melt the butter on a low heat.
  2. Then add 1 cup Kaju or 120 grams whole cashews.
  3. Stir and sauté the cashews till they become golden on low to medium-low heat.
  4. Remove them on a plate and keep them aside.
  1. In the same pan, there will be some butter remaining. Add 1 tej patta (Indian bay leaf) and sauté for a few seconds on low heat.
  2. Then add 1.5 cups roughly chopped tomatoes and ½ cup water (about 4 medium or 3 large tomatoes). Stir and mix well and simmer the mixture on medium to high heat till the tomatoes soften. You can also cover the pan and cook the tomatoes.
  1. When the tomatoes are simmering, take 18 to 20 cashews in a grinder or blender.
  2. Powder them to a very fine texture. If fat is released while grinding or blending cashews, it is alright. Scrape the sides and powder them finely.
  3. Remove and keep the ground cashews aside.
  1. Meanwhile, the tomatoes have softened. Switch off the heat and allow the tomatoes to become warm or cool down completely.
  2. Discard the tej patta and add the tomatoes to the same grinder or blender jar in which you ground the cashews.
  3. Blend the tomatoes into a smooth puree. Keep aside.
  1. In the same pan in which you sautéed the cashews and cooked the tomatoes, heat 2 tablespoons of butter on low heat.
  2. Add 1 teaspoon ginger-garlic paste (made from 1 inch ginger and 3 to 4 garlic cloves, crushed). Stir and sauté for a few seconds or till the raw aroma of ginger-garlic goes away.
  3. Then add the cashew powder.
  4. Stir and mix to combine.
  5. On a low heat, keep stirring the cashew powder so that it cooks uniformly. You have to stir continuously, otherwise the cashew powder sticks to the pan and can get burned or too browned.
  6. Stir till the color of the cashew powder changes to a pale light golden and you get a nice aroma. However, don’t brown the cashew powder.
  7. Then add the prepared tomato puree.
  8. Stir and mix very well.
  9. Sauté the tomato puree for about 4 minutes on low to medium heat. Keep stirring often.
  10. Then add ½ teaspoon Kashmiri red chili powder or deghi mirch. Stir and mix very well.
  11. Add ⅔ to ¾ cup water or as required. Stir and mix again.
  12. Add 1 to 2 slit green chilies.
  13. Simmer the gravy for 3 to 4 minutes on low to medium heat.
  1. Then add the sautéed golden cashews. Stir. You can also keep a few cashews for garnish.
  2. Season with salt as required.
  3. Add ¼ to ½ teaspoon sugar. Stir and simmer the curry for a further 2 minutes.
  4. Lastly, add the following ingredients:
    • ½ teaspoon garam masala powder
    • 2 to 3 tablespoons low-fat cream or light cream or cooking cream, or 1 to 2 tablespoons whipping cream
    • 1 teaspoon kasuri methi (dried fenugreek leaves), crushed
  5. Stir again till the entire cream is mixed well with the kaju masala curry. Switch off the flame. Check the seasoning and add more salt or sugar if required.
  6. Garnish with coriander leaves. Serve kaju masala with tandoori roti, naan, phulka, or paratha. You can also serve it with cumin rice, veg pulao, steamed basmati rice, saffron rice, or ghee rice.
  7. The leftovers can be refrigerated in an airtight container and used within a day.

Recipe Tips

You can adjust the quantity of water based on the type and quality of rice used. If using a small grinder or mixer, add a bit of water to make a paste of ginger, garlic, and green chilies. For best results, use a deep, thick-bottomed pot or pan to ensure even cooking.
