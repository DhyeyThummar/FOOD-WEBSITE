Kadhi

Prep Time

15 minutes

Cook Time

20 minutes

Total Time

35 minutes

Servings

4 people

Ingredients

    Kadhi Recipe
    1. In a bowl, take 1.5 cups sour curd or about 375 grams of curd. Whisk it well until smooth.
    2. Add the following ingredients to the whisked curd:
      • 8 tablespoons gram flour (besan)
      • ½ teaspoon red chili powder
      • ½ teaspoon turmeric powder
      • ½ teaspoon garam masala powder
      • 1 teaspoon salt
    3. Stir and mix everything again.
    4. Add 3 cups water and stir again.
    5. Stir very well to make a smooth mixture without lumps. If there are lumps, break them with a wire whisk, spatula, or with your fingers. Set the curd slurry aside.
    6. You can use a blender to make this mixture, but avoid over-blending to prevent making butter instead of a smooth blended curd.
    1. Take 1 cup gram flour (besan) in a bowl and add the following ingredients:
      • ½ teaspoon ajwain (carom seeds)
      • ½ teaspoon red chili powder or cayenne pepper
      • ½ teaspoon garam masala powder
      • ⅔ teaspoon salt (or as required)

Steps to Make Recipe

  1. Take 1 cup gram flour (besan) in a bowl.
  2. Add 1 cup thinly sliced onions (about 2 medium to large onions, approximately 150 grams of onions).
  3. Mix everything well and keep aside covered for 30 minutes.
  4. This will allow the onions to release their water in the mixture. Depending on the water content in the onions, the mixture will become very moist or just about moist. Mix again.
  5. Then accordingly add water as required to make a thick batter. I added ¼ cup water. You can also skip adding water if the mixture is too moist.
  6. Heat oil for deep frying in a pan or kadai. You can use any neutral-flavored oil with a high smoking temperature or mustard oil. When the oil becomes medium hot, use a spoon to drop the pakora batter into the oil.
  7. When the pakoras are partly cooked, turn them over with a slotted spoon and continue to fry the other side.
  8. Fry until the pakoras are crisp and golden, turning over a couple of times for even cooking.
  9. Remove the fried pakoras and place them on a kitchen paper towel to absorb extra oil. Fry the pakoras in batches. Once done, keep all of them aside.
  1. In another pan or kadai, heat 2 tablespoons of mustard oil. Use a large-bottomed pot to avoid spillage while cooking. If you don’t have mustard oil, you can use any neutral-flavored oil, though traditional Punjabi kadhi uses mustard oil.
  2. Add 1 teaspoon cumin seeds, 8 to 10 fenugreek seeds (methi seeds), and a generous pinch of asafoetida (hing). Allow the cumin seeds to crackle and the fenugreek seeds to change color. Fry on a low flame to prevent burning the spices.
  3. Add ⅓ cup chopped onions. Stir and sauté for 3 minutes on low to medium-low heat.
  4. Add 1 tablespoon chopped ginger, ¾ to 1 tablespoon chopped garlic, and 2 chopped green chilies. Stir and sauté for a minute.
  5. Add 8 to 10 curry leaves and 2 dry red chilies (halved or broken).
  6. Stir and sauté for a minute on low heat.
  7. Add the prepared curd slurry.
  8. Stir very well.
  9. Increase the flame to medium and bring the kadhi to a boil, stirring often to prevent the bottom from burning. The cooking time will be around 14 to 16 minutes.
  10. Once it boils, lower the heat and simmer for a further 6 to 7 minutes. Stir occasionally. The kadhi will thicken as it simmers. If it becomes too thick, add some hot water.
  11. Add the onion pakoras to the kadhi. Stir gently.
  12. Cover with a lid and let the pakoras soak in the kadhi for 8 to 10 minutes.
  13. Sprinkle some garam masala powder on the Punjabi Kadhi.
  14. Garnish with coriander leaves and serve hot with steamed rice or jeera rice, topped with a few teaspoons of ghee. You can also serve it with roti or paratha. Kadhi Chawal (kadhi with steamed rice) is a popular and delicious combination.

Recipe Tips

You can adjust the quantity of water based on the type and quality of rice used. If using a small grinder or mixer, add a bit of water to make a paste of ginger, garlic, and green chilies. For best results, use a deep, thick-bottomed pot or pan to ensure even cooking.
