Paneer Butter Masala

Prep Time

15 minutes

Cook Time

20 minutes

Total Time

35 minutes

Servings

4 people

Ingredients

    Hyderabadi Veg Biryani Ingredients
    • 18 to 20 cashews, soaked in ⅓ cup hot water for 20 to 30 minutes
    • 2 cups tomatoes, diced or roughly chopped (for tomato puree)
    • 1 inch piece of ginger, peeled and crushed
    • 3 to 4 small to medium-sized garlic cloves, crushed
    • 200 to 250 grams paneer, sliced into cubes
    • 2 to 3 tablespoons fresh water (for blending the cashew paste)

    For Tomato Gravy

    • 2 to 3 tablespoons butter (or 1 tablespoon oil + 1-2 tablespoons butter)
    • 1 medium-sized tej patta (bay leaf)
    • 1 teaspoon ginger-garlic paste (or the freshly made paste from step 3)
    • 1 teaspoon Kashmiri red chili powder (or adjust to taste with ½ teaspoon to 1 teaspoon based on preference)
    • 1.5 cups water
    • 1-inch ginger, julienne (reserve some for garnish)
    • 1 or 2 slit green chilies (optional)
    • Salt (to taste)
    • ¼ to 1 teaspoon sugar (optional, adjust based on the sourness of tomatoes)
    • 1 teaspoon crushed dry fenugreek leaves (kasuri methi) (optional)
    • 1 teaspoon garam masala

    For Cream

    • 2 to 3 tablespoons low-fat or light cream (or 1 to 2 tablespoons heavy whipping cream)

    Garnish

    • 1 to 2 tablespoons chopped coriander leaves (cilantro)
    • Remaining ginger julienne (for garnish)

    Notes:

    • Butter: You can use salted or unsalted butter based on your preference.
    • Spices: Adjust the quantity of red chili powder and garam masala to suit your taste.
    • Cream: Adjust the amount of cream based on how rich you want the curry to be.

Steps to Make Recipe

  1. Soak 18 to 20 cashews in ⅓ cup hot water for 20 to 30 minutes.
  2. While the cashews are soaking, prep the other ingredients: chop tomatoes, prepare ginger-garlic paste, and slice paneer into cubes.
  3. To make the ginger-garlic paste, crush a 1-inch piece of peeled ginger with 3 to 4 small to medium-sized garlic cloves in a mortar & pestle until you get a semi-fine paste. Set it aside.
    Note: Do not add any water while crushing ginger & garlic.
  4. After 20 to 30 minutes, drain the cashews and add them to a blender with 2 to 3 tablespoons of fresh water. Blend to a smooth paste.
  5. Remove the cashew paste from the blender and set it aside.
  6. In the same blender, add 2 cups of diced or roughly chopped tomatoes and blend to a smooth puree. Set aside.
    Note: There's no need to blanch the tomatoes or wash the blender before blending.
  7. Heat a thick-bottomed or heavy pan over low to medium-low heat. Add 2 tablespoons of butter (or 3 to 4 tablespoons for a richer version).
    Tip: You can add 1 tablespoon oil + 1-2 tablespoons butter to prevent browning.
  8. Add 1 medium-sized tej patta and fry for 2 to 3 seconds until fragrant.
  9. Add the ginger-garlic paste and sauté for 10 to 12 seconds until the raw aroma disappears.
  10. Pour in the prepared tomato puree carefully, as it may splatter.
  11. Mix the puree well with the butter and cook on low to medium-low heat, stirring occasionally.
  12. Simmer the puree for 5 to 6 minutes.
  13. Add 1 teaspoon Kashmiri red chili powder or deghi mirch, or adjust according to taste, and mix well.
  14. Sauté the tomato puree until the butter leaves the sides of the pan and the mixture thickens into a paste (about 14 to 17 minutes).
  15. Add the prepared cashew paste and mix well. Continue stirring and sautéing on low to medium-low heat.
  16. Sauté for 3 to 4 minutes until the cashew paste is cooked and the oil begins to separate from the masala.
  17. Add 1.5 cups of water and mix well.
  18. Let the curry simmer and come to a boil, stirring occasionally.
  19. Add ginger julienne (reserving some for garnish) and stir. The curry will start thickening.
  20. After 2 to 3 minutes, add 1 or 2 slit green chilies.
  21. Add salt to taste and, if needed, ¼ to 1 teaspoon of sugar depending on the sourness of the tomatoes.
    Tip: If you plan to add cream, you may need less sugar, or you can skip the sugar entirely.
  22. Mix well and simmer for a minute.
  23. Once the gravy reaches the desired consistency, add 200 to 250 grams of paneer cubes.
  24. Stir gently to mix the paneer into the gravy. You can switch off the heat at this point.
  25. Add 1 teaspoon crushed dry fenugreek leaves (kasuri methi) and 1 teaspoon garam masala.
  26. Stir in 2 to 3 tablespoons of low-fat cream or 1 to 2 tablespoons of heavy whipping cream.
  27. Stir gently and switch off the heat if you haven't already.
  28. Serve Paneer Butter Masala hot, garnished with 1 to 2 tablespoons of chopped coriander leaves (cilantro) and the reserved ginger julienne. You can also drizzle some cream or dot with butter while serving. Enjoy!

Recipe Tips

You can adjust the quantity of water based on the type and quality of rice used. If using a small grinder or mixer, add a bit of water to make a paste of ginger, garlic, and green chilies. For best results, use a deep, thick-bottomed pot or pan to ensure even cooking.
