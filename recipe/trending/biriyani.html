Veg Biriyani

Prep Time

15 minutes

Cook Time

20 minutes

Total Time

35 minutes

Servings

4 people

Ingredients

  • For Soaking Rice:
  • 1.5 cups basmati rice (300 grams)
  • For Prep Vegetables:
  • 3 cups mixed vegetables (including green peas, or your choice of mixed veggies)
  • 1 large onion (about 1 heaped cup thinly sliced)
  • 10 grams ginger (2 pieces of 2-inch ginger or 1.5 tablespoons finely chopped ginger)
  • 5 grams garlic (10 to 12 medium garlic cloves or 1 tablespoon finely chopped garlic)
  • 3 to 4 green chilies (slit)
  • ⅓ cup chopped cilantro (coriander leaves)
  • ⅓ cup chopped mint leaves
  • For Cooking Rice:
  • 5 cups water
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 tej patta (bay leaf)
  • 3 green cardamoms
  • 3 cloves
  • 1 black cardamom
  • 1 inch true cinnamon or cassia cinnamon
  • 2 single strands of mace
  • For Making Biryani Gravy:
  • 3 tablespoons ghee
  • 1 teaspoon shah jeera (caraway seeds)
  • 1 tej patta (bay leaf)
  • 3 green cardamoms
  • 3 cloves
  • 1 black cardamom
  • 1 inch cinnamon stick
  • 1 cup fresh curd or yogurt
  • ½ teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder or cayenne
  • 2 tablespoons cashews
  • 1 tablespoon raisins
  • 2 tablespoons almonds (blanched or raw)
  • Salt (to taste)
  • ½ cup water (for pressure cooking) or ¾ cup water (if cooking in a pot)
  • For Assembling and Baking:
  • 4 to 5 tablespoons milk
  • ¼ teaspoon saffron strands
  • 2 teaspoons rose water or kewra water (optional)
  • ⅓ to ½ cup golden fried onions (birista) (optional)
  • Additional Notes:v

Steps to Make Recipe

  1. Soak Rice:
    1. Rinse 1.5 cups of basmati rice (300 grams) in clean water until the water runs clear of starch. Use a bowl or colander to rinse the rice. Soak the rice grains in water for 30 minutes after rinsing.
    2. Tip: Use aged basmati rice or sella basmati rice (parboiled basmati rice) for the best results.
    3. After 30 minutes, drain all the water and set the rice aside.
  2. Prep Vegetables:
    1. While the rice is soaking, rinse, peel, and chop all the veggies to speed up the cooking process.
    2. Ingredients:
      • 3 cups mixed vegetables, including green peas.
      • 1 large onion, thinly sliced (about 1 heaped cup).
      • 10 grams ginger, finely chopped or crushed (about 2 pieces of 2-inch ginger or 1.5 tablespoons finely chopped ginger; 1 tablespoon ginger paste).
      • 5 grams garlic, finely chopped or crushed (10 to 12 medium garlic cloves or 1 tablespoon finely chopped garlic; ½ tablespoon garlic paste).
      • 3 to 4 green chilies, slit.
      • ⅓ cup chopped coriander leaves (cilantro) and mint leaves.
  3. Cook Rice:
    1. Use your preferred method to cook the rice (microwave, pressure cooker, instant pot, or pot). If using a pot, add 5 cups of water.
    2. When the water is hot, add the following spices and 1 teaspoon of salt:
      • 1 tej patta (bay leaf)
      • 3 green cardamoms
      • 3 cloves
      • 1 black cardamom
      • 1 inch cinnamon stick
      • 2 single strands of mace
    3. Bring the water to a boil over high heat.
    4. Add the soaked basmati rice to the boiling water.
    5. Gently stir with a spoon or fork. Check the taste of the water to ensure it is slightly salty.
    6. Tip: Add more salt if the water is not slightly salty.
    7. Do not reduce the heat; cook the rice until it is about 75% or ¾ cooked.
    8. Tip: The rice should still have a slight bite to it. Drain the semi-cooked rice in a colander, and rinse gently with water to stop the cooking process.
  4. Make Biryani Gravy:
    1. Heat 3 tablespoons of ghee in a 2-litre pressure cooker or pan. Add the following whole spices and sauté until they crackle:
      • 1 teaspoon shah jeera (caraway seeds)
      • 1 tej patta (bay leaf)
      • 3 green cardamoms
      • 3 cloves
      • 1 black cardamom
      • 1 inch cinnamon stick
    2. Add the sliced onions and sauté on low to medium heat. Add a pinch of salt to speed up cooking. Stir frequently to cook evenly.
    3. While the onions cook, beat 1 cup of fresh curd or yogurt until smooth.
    4. Sauté the onions until golden brown or caramelized, being careful not to burn them.
    5. Add the ginger-garlic paste and sliced green chilies (or finely chopped ginger and garlic if preferred). Sauté until the raw aroma disappears.
    6. Add ½ teaspoon turmeric powder and 1 teaspoon red chili powder or cayenne. Stir and mix well.
    7. Add the chopped vegetables and mix thoroughly. Sauté for 1 to 2 minutes.
    8. Lower the heat and add the beaten yogurt or curd. Stir immediately.
    9. Add ½ cup of water (¾ cup if using a pot). Season with salt to taste and mix well.
    10. Pressure cook for 1 whistle or 3 to 4 minutes on medium heat. If using a pot, cook until the vegetables are tender, but avoid overcooking.
    11. Once the pressure settles, check the gravy. If vegetables are undercooked, simmer without the lid until tender. If there is excess water, simmer until some water evaporates. The gravy should be medium or slightly thick in consistency.
    12. Add 2 tablespoons cashews, 1 tablespoon raisins, and 2 tablespoons almonds (blanched or raw).
      • Tip: Check the salt and add more if needed. Chop the nuts before adding if desired.
    13. Mix well and set aside.
    14. Warm 4 to 5 tablespoons of milk and add ¼ teaspoon saffron strands. Stir and set aside.
  5. Assemble and Layer Veg Dum Biryani:
    1. In a thick-bottomed pan, layer half of the vegetable curry.
    2. Top with half of the semi-cooked rice.
    3. Sprinkle half of the chopped coriander leaves, mint leaves, and saffron-infused milk.
    4. Add the remaining vegetable gravy.
    5. Top with the remaining rice and sprinkle the remaining coriander leaves, mint leaves, and saffron milk. Optionally, add 2 teaspoons of rose water or kewra water (pandanus extract).
    6. Tip: You can add ⅓ to ½ cup of golden fried onions (birista) if available. You can make 2 layers or up to 4 layers, but remember to keep rice as the top layer and vegetable gravy as the bottom layer.
  6. Baking Biryani:
    1. Preheat the oven to 180°C (350°F) for 15 minutes. Bake the veg biryani in a preheated oven for 30 to 40 minutes.
    2. Use an oven-safe glass bowl, casserole dish, or Dutch oven. Assemble the biryani in the dish, seal tightly with aluminum foil or a heat-resistant lid, and bake.
  7. Check Hyderabadi Dum Biryani:
    1. After 30 minutes, switch off the flame and check the bottom layer of the biryani with a fork or spoon. There should be no liquids at the bottom.
    2. If there are still liquids, continue to cook. After the dum cooking, let the biryani rest for 5 to 7 minutes before serving.
    3. When serving, make sure to equally distribute both the vegetables and rice layers.

Recipe Tips

Ensure to have an oven-safe glass bowl or casserole dish or a dutch oven for baking. Preheat the oven to 180°C (350°F) and bake the assembled biryani for 30 to 40 minutes. Use aluminum foil or an oven-safe lid to seal the dish while baking.
