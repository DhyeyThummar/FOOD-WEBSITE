Eggless Vanilla Cake

Prep Time

15 minutes

Cook Time

20 minutes

Total Time

35 minutes

Servings

4 people

Ingredients

    Hyderabadi Veg Biryani Ingredients

    For the Cake:

    • All-Purpose Flour: 1.5 cups
    • Baking Powder: 1 teaspoon
    • Salt: A pinch
    • Butter: Slightly more than ½ cup (to be melted)
    • Raw Sugar (or White Sugar): ¾ cup
    • Vanilla Extract: 1 teaspoon
    • Curd (Yogurt): ¼ cup
    • Apple Cider Vinegar: 1 tablespoon (or white vinegar or lemon juice as substitutes)
    • Water: ½ cup
    • Baking Soda: ½ teaspoon

    For the Jam Glaze Icing:

    • Fruit Jam: ½ cup (any sweet fruit jam, e.g., strawberry jam)
    • Tapioca Starch (or Arrowroot Flour or Cornstarch): 1 tablespoon
    • Water: 3 tablespoons (or more if needed)
    • Butter: 1 tablespoon

    Optional Decoration:

    • Unsweetened Desiccated Coconut: For sprinkling on top (optional)

Steps to Make Recipe

Before you make the eggless vanilla cake recipe, get all your ingredients ready. Make sure all of them are at room temperature, except the butter that can be cold or straightaway removed from the refrigerator.

  1. First add 1.5 cups of all-purpose flour, 1 tsp of baking powder, and a pinch of salt into a sieve placed over a mixing bowl.
  2. Sift the dry ingredients once or twice. Set aside.
  3. Grease a 7.5 or 8-inch round pan with some butter. You can line the pan with parchment if you prefer.
  4. Heat slightly more than ½ cup of butter in a small pan or bowl on medium-low heat until it just melts.
  5. Measure the melted butter in a ½ cup measuring cup. Set aside. Preheat your oven to 190°C (375°F) for 15 to 20 minutes before baking.
  6. In another bowl, add ¾ cup of raw sugar (or white sugar), 1 teaspoon of vanilla extract, ¼ cup of curd (yogurt), and 1 tablespoon of apple cider vinegar.
  7. Note: Beat the curd separately in a bowl and then add. Also, use fresh curd and not sour curd.
  8. Tip: Swap with white vinegar or lemon juice if you do not have apple cider vinegar.
  9. Add ½ cup of water.
  10. Whisk briskly until all of the sugar is dissolved and the curd gets evenly mixed. It should be like buttermilk, slightly off-color and foamy.
  11. It is important that you beat the curd before adding as mentioned above so that you don’t have tiny lumps of curd in this liquid mixture.
  12. Now add ½ teaspoon of baking soda to the wet mixture.
  13. Stir until the baking soda is mixed evenly. The baking soda will cause some bubbling in this wet liquid mixture.

Make Cake Batter

  1. Add the liquid mixture and melted butter to the dry sifted ingredients.
  2. Mix very well with a wired whisk. Break apart any lumps by tapping them with the whisk. Be careful not to over-mix the batter.
  3. It should be a smooth batter.
  4. Tip: If the batter becomes too thick or looks dry, add some more milk or water (at room temperature) to get it to the desired consistency. Depending on the flour texture and quality, you may need to adjust the liquid.
  5. Pour the batter into the greased pan. Tap the pan on the countertop a few times so that any extra air bubbles are released and the batter spreads itself evenly in the pan.
  6. You do not need to even the batter with a spatula. The batter spreads on its own.

Bake Eggless Vanilla Cake

  1. Place the cake pan in the preheated oven on the middle rack. If using an OTG or a regular oven, heat both the top and bottom heating elements.
  2. Bake at 180°C (375°F) for 30 to 35 minutes, or until the top crust is golden.
  3. Tip 1: The baking time varies with different oven types and temperatures. Keep an eye on the cake as it bakes so that it does not brown too much.
  4. Tip 2: Remember not to open the oven door until ¾ of the cake is baked. Opening and closing the oven door many times can cause the cake to sink or collapse.
  5. Tip 3: When the cake begins to get golden and you see that it is browning very quickly, cover the top with aluminum foil or butter paper and continue to bake.
  6. Check the doneness of the cake with a bamboo skewer. It should be clean with no sticky batter on it.
  7. Place the pan on a wire rack and let the cake cool to room temperature. Let the cake cool completely before you spread the jam glaze on it.

Make Jam Glaze Icing

  1. First, add ½ cup of any sweet fruit jam of your choice in a small skillet or pan.
  2. Add 1 tablespoon of tapioca starch (or arrowroot flour or cornstarch), plus 3 tablespoons of water (or more if needed) and 1 tablespoon of butter.
  3. Place the pan on low heat and begin to mix.
  4. Keep stirring until all of the ingredients have melted together and are evenly combined.
  5. Simmer on low heat for 3 to 4 minutes until the glaze thickens, stirring often. Do not boil or cook the mixture down too much.
  6. Set aside and allow the jam glaze to cool slightly, so that it is warm and not hot.

Frost Cake

  1. When the cake cools, remove it gently from the pan. Place it on a board, tray, plate, or cake stand. A rotating cake stand makes applying the glaze easier.
  2. Place some of the jam icing on top and use a spatula to spread the glaze evenly on the top and sides of the cake.
  3. Sprinkle some unsweetened desiccated coconut on top if desired. You can also spread it on the sides.
  4. Cover with a large steel bowl, taking care that the coconut and icing do not touch the bowl. Let the glaze cool completely and set.
  5. Later slice and serve. You can also refrigerate for 1 to 2 hours and then serve.
  6. Note: The butter solidifies when cooled in the refrigerator, making the cake slightly dense. Let the cake come to room temperature before serving, or warm it lightly in your oven at 90°C (195°F) for a few minutes.

Recipe Tips

Ingredient Temperatures: Butter: Can be melted cold or at room temperature. Curd/Yogurt: Ensure it’s fresh and not sour for the best results. Mixing Tips: Avoid Over-Mixing: When combining the wet and dry ingredients, mix just until smooth to avoid a dense cake. Consistency Check: If the batter is too thick, adjust with a bit more water or milk. Baking Tips: Oven Variability: Oven temperatures can vary, so check for doneness a few minutes before the suggested time. Prevent Over-Browning: Cover with aluminum foil if the top is browning too quickly.
