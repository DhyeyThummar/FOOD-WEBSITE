Moist Eggless Chocolate Cake

Prep Time

15 minutes

Cook Time

20 minutes

Total Time

35 minutes

Servings

4 people

Ingredients

    Hyderabadi Veg Biryani Ingredients

    For the Cake:

    • Whole Wheat Flour (or All-Purpose Flour): 1 cup
    • Cocoa Powder: 3 tablespoons
    • Salt: A pinch
    • Baking Soda: ½ teaspoon
    • Sugar: ¾ cup (raw cane sugar or regular white sugar)
    • Cold Water: 1 cup
    • Oil: ¼ cup (sunflower or any neutral oil)
    • Lemon Juice: 1 tablespoon (or apple cider vinegar, white vinegar)
    • Vanilla Extract: ½ teaspoon (or chocolate extract/essence)

    For the Cocoa Frosting:

    • Sunflower Oil: 1 tablespoon
    • Almond Milk: ¼ cup (or any dairy or non-dairy milk)
    • Raw Sugar: ¼ cup (or regular sugar)
    • Cocoa Powder: 3 tablespoons

Steps to Make Recipe

  1. First grease a round baking pan of 7.5 inches diameter x 2 inches height with oil. Preheat your oven to 200 degrees Celsius (392 degrees Fahrenheit) for 15 minutes.
  2. Sieve 1 cup whole wheat flour (you can also use all-purpose flour) and 3 tablespoons cocoa powder. Keep a plate or tray below the sieve.
  3. Add a pinch of salt and ½ teaspoon baking soda.
  4. Sift the ingredients on to a plate.
  5. Keep the sieved dry ingredients aside.
  6. In another pan or bowl, add ¾ cup sugar. I have used raw, unrefined cane sugar, however regular white sugar can also be used. Add 1 cup cold water. The water has to be very cold.
  7. Stir with a small whisk till the sugar dissolves.
  8. Now add ¼ cup oil. I added sunflower oil.
  9. Note: I would not suggest adding butter as a few readers did tell me that the butter solidifies and it becomes difficult to get it to a homogenous mixture. Stick with oils that are not solid at room temperature for this cake.
  10. Stir briskly so that everything is mixed well.
  11. It should be a homogenous mixture.
  12. Next add 1 tablespoon lemon juice. Stir again.
  13. Tip: No lemon? No problem. We just need something acidic here. You could use 1 tablespoon of apple cider vinegar or white vinegar in place of lemon juice.
  14. Now add ½ teaspoon vanilla extract. Feel free to add chocolate extract or chocolate essence instead of vanilla.
  15. Mix very well.
  16. Add the sieved dry ingredients to the wet mixture.
  17. Using a wired whisk, mix everything well.
  18. There should be no lumps in the cake batter. The batter should also be slightly thin, not thick like a brownie batter.
  19. Pour the eggless chocolate cake batter into the prepared cake pan.
  20. Tap the sides so that the extra air bubbles are let out.
  21. Bake the eggless chocolate cake in a preheated oven for 200 degrees Celsius (392 degrees Fahrenheit) for 35 to 40 mins.
  22. Tip: For baking in a convection mode of a microwave oven, preheat the oven to 180 degrees Celsius (355 degrees Fahrenheit) for 15 minutes. Then bake at 180 degrees Celsius (355 degrees Fahrenheit) for 30 to 35 minutes.
  23. Note: Since temperatures vary from oven to oven be sure to keep an eye on it. Do not open the oven door many times as this will cause your chocolate cake to sink in the center. Only open the door after three-fourth of the baking is done.
  24. The temperature varies widely from oven to oven, which is why I recommend getting a stand-alone oven thermometer. Generally, it takes 35 minutes for this cake to bake. But if your oven is very hot or cool, it will take less or more time for baking.
  25. For doneness check the chocolate cake with a bamboo skewer or toothpick. The bamboo skewer should come out clean.
  26. If the toothpick is sticky with the batter, then place the chocolate cake back in the oven and continue to bake for some more minutes.
  27. Once the eggless chocolate cake cools down enough to handle, remove it from the baking tin and place it on a wired rack to finish cooling. Before frosting, let the cake cool completely.
  28. This frosting recipe makes for more of a thin chocolate glaze. Take 1 tablespoon sunflower oil, ¼ cup almond milk, and ¼ cup raw sugar in a small saucepan.
  29. Tip: If you prefer a slightly thick frosting, reduce the almond milk to just 2 tablespoons. You can substitute any dairy or alternative milk in place of almond milk.
  30. Begin to heat the pan on a low flame. Mix very well, continuing to stir, so that the sugar is dissolved into the milk. Do not boil the almond milk, but just make it lightly hot enough to dissolve the sugar.
  31. When the almond milk is just lightly hot and after all the sugar has been dissolved, add 3 tablespoons cocoa powder.
  32. With a small wired whisk, mix and stir so that the cocoa powder gets mixed evenly.
  33. The cocoa frosting is ready now. Switch off the flame.
  34. While still hot, pour the frosting on the top and sides of the eggless chocolate cake.
  35. Tip: Usually I wrap a medium-sized plate with aluminum foil and then place the cake on it. This makes the frosting work neater.
  36. With an offset spatula, spread the icing evenly all over on top and the sides of the eggless chocolate cake. If you want a layer cake, you can slice the chocolate cake in half and spread frosting in the center and then on the top.
  37. Keep the vegan chocolate cake covered in the fridge for the frosting to set for a couple of hours. I like to use a dome-shaped bowl to cover the cake. Make sure there is enough space between the cake and the bowl which you are using for covering.
  38. The frosting will set after a few hours. I kept it in the fridge overnight.
  39. For a little added pizzazz, feel free to top your cake with some simple decor. I recommend chocolate shavings, sprinkles, or sliced nuts. If they are in season, fresh berries are also a nice complement to chocolate cake.
  40. If you want to make a more intricate design, try holding a doily over the top of the cake and sift icing sugar or cocoa powder over the top to look like lace.
  41. The eggless chocolate cake is ready to be served. Slice and enjoy!

Recipe Tips

Flour Choice: Whole wheat flour adds a slightly nutty flavor and denser texture, while all-purpose flour results in a lighter cake. Choose based on your preference. Sweeteners: Raw cane sugar gives a richer flavor but you can use regular white sugar as an alternative. Liquid Temperature: Ensure the cold water is truly cold to help the baking soda react properly and leaven the cake. Oil vs. Butter: As mentioned, use neutral oils (like sunflower) rather than butter to avoid issues with solidification. Baking Powder: For a fluffier cake, you can substitute 1 teaspoon of baking powder for the baking soda. Adjust the quantity if you make changes to the acidic ingredient.
