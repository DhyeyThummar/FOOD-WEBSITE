Banana Cake

Prep Time

15 minutes

Cook Time

20 minutes

Total Time

35 minutes

Servings

4 people

Ingredients

    Hyderabadi Veg Biryani Ingredients

    For the Banana Puree:

    • Ripe or Over-Ripe Bananas: 4 medium to large
    • Sugar: ½ cup
    • Neutral Flavored Oil: ⅔ cup (e.g., vegetable oil, canola oil)
    • Vanilla Extract: 1 teaspoon

    For the Cake Batter:

    • Whole Wheat Flour: 1½ cups
    • Baking Powder: 1 teaspoon
    • Baking Soda: ½ teaspoon
    • Salt: A pinch
    • Walnuts: 10 halved and finely chopped (optional; can substitute with raisins, pistachios, or other nuts/dry fruits)

    For Baking:

    • Grease or Line: A loaf pan (7.5 x 4 x 2.5 inches) or round cake pan (7.5 x 2 inches)

Steps to Make Recipe

  1. Make Banana Puree:
    1. Mash 4 medium to large ripe or over-ripe bananas with a fork to a smooth consistency. (You can use a blender for a smoother puree if needed.)
    2. Add ½ cup of sugar, ⅔ cup of any neutral flavored oil, and 1 teaspoon of vanilla extract to the mashed bananas.
    3. Stir well so the oil mixes with the banana puree and the sugar dissolves completely.
  2. Make Batter:
    1. Sieve 1½ cups of whole wheat flour, 1 teaspoon of baking powder, ½ teaspoon of baking soda, and a pinch of salt directly into the bowl.
    2. Fold the dry ingredients thoroughly into the wet ingredients using the cut and fold method.
    3. If the batter looks too thick, add some water, almond milk, or lite coconut milk to adjust the consistency. Only add extra liquid if necessary.
    4. Add 10 halved walnuts that have been finely chopped and fold them gently into the batter.
  3. Bake the Cake:
    1. Pour the batter into a greased or lined loaf pan (7.5 x 4 x 2.5 inches) or round cake pan (7.5 x 2 inches).
    2. Bake in a preheated oven at 350°F (180°C) for 45 to 50 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the cake comes out clean. Baking time may vary by oven, so monitor closely.
    3. If the top of the cake starts browning too much, cover it with aluminum foil or parchment paper while baking.
    4. For a stove-top pressure cooker method, follow the detailed instructions in the Pressure Cooker Cake post.
  4. Serve:
    1. Allow the cake to cool before slicing.
    2. Serve plain with your tea or coffee.

Recipe Tips

Banana Ripeness: The riper the bananas, the sweeter and more flavorful the puree will be. Over-ripe bananas work best for this recipe. Consistency Adjustments: If the batter is too thick, gradually add water, almond milk, or lite coconut milk until the desired consistency is reached. Substitutes for Nuts: If you prefer, you can substitute walnuts with raisins, pistachios, or other nuts and dried fruits according to your taste. Texture Tip: For a smoother batter, use a blender to mash the bananas if you find it difficult to get a smooth consistency with a fork. Oven Variability: Baking times may vary based on your oven. Always check for doneness with a toothpick, and adjust baking time if necessary.
