Butter cake

Prep Time

15 minutes

Cook Time

20 minutes

Total Time

35 minutes

Servings

4 people

    Hyderabadi Veg Biryani Ingredients

    For Sifting Dry Ingredients:

    • All-Purpose Flour: 1.5 leveled cups (about 188 grams)
    • Salt: ⅛ teaspoon (2 to 3 pinches) – omit if using salted butter
    • Baking Powder: 1.5 leveled teaspoons

    For the Cake Batter:

    • Butter: 200 grams (about ¾ cup), softened (use unsalted butter)
    • Sweetened Condensed Milk: 400 grams (1 can)
    • Vanilla Extract: 1 teaspoon (or ½ teaspoon vanilla essence)
    • Milk: ¼ cup (optional, to adjust batter consistency)
    1. Prepare Dry Ingredients:
      1. Sift:
        • All-Purpose Flour: 1.5 leveled cups
        • Salt: ⅛ teaspoon (omit if using salted butter)
        • Baking Powder: 1.5 leveled teaspoons
      2. Sift these ingredients together into a bowl and set aside.
    2. Prep Pan:
      1. Grease a loaf pan (8.5 x 4.5 x 2.5 inches) with butter. Optionally, line the pan with parchment or butter paper and grease it.
      2. Preheat the oven to 170°C (335°F).
    3. Whip Butter:
      1. Butter: 200 grams, softened
      2. Mix using a stand mixer with a paddle attachment on high speed (9 to 10) until light and fluffy. Stop to scrape the sides occasionally.
    4. Combine Ingredients:
      1. Add:
        • Sweetened Condensed Milk: 400 grams
        • Vanilla Extract: 1 teaspoon (or ½ teaspoon vanilla essence)
      2. Mix on low speed (1) until well blended. Avoid over-mixing.
    5. Make Cake Batter:
      1. Add the sifted dry ingredients to the wet mixture.
      2. Mix lightly with a spatula, then blend on low speed (1) until just combined. If the batter is too thick, add up to ¼ cup milk as needed.
    6. Bake the Cake:
      1. Pour the batter into the prepared loaf pan.
      2. Shake the pan to level the batter.
      3. Bake at 170°C (335°F) in a convection oven or at 180°C (355°F) in a non-convection oven. Cover with aluminum foil if the top browns too quickly.
      4. Bake for 50 to 60 minutes or until a toothpick comes out clean.
      5. Cool on a wire rack, then slice when completely cooled.
    7. Serve:
      • Optional: Sprinkle powdered sugar or icing sugar on top before serving. Use a tea strainer to sift the sugar over the cake.

Steps to Make Recipe

For Sifting Dry Ingredients:

  • All-Purpose Flour: 1.5 leveled cups (about 188 grams)
  • Salt: ⅛ teaspoon (2 to 3 pinches) – omit if using salted butter
  • Baking Powder: 1.5 leveled teaspoons

For the Cake Batter:

  • Butter: 200 grams (about ¾ cup), softened (use unsalted butter)
  • Sweetened Condensed Milk: 400 grams (1 can)
  • Vanilla Extract: 1 teaspoon (or ½ teaspoon vanilla essence)
  • Milk: ¼ cup (optional, to adjust batter consistency)
  1. Sift Dry Ingredients:
    1. Take all the ingredients and keep them ready:
      • 200 grams butter (about ¾ of a cup)
      • 400 grams sweetened condensed milk (or 1 can)
      • 1.5 leveled cups all-purpose flour (or 188 grams)
      • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
    2. Keep a sieve on top of a bowl. Add the all-purpose flour in the sieve.
    3. Add ⅛ teaspoon salt (2 to 3 pinches). If using salted butter, then skip adding salt.
    4. Add 1.5 leveled teaspoons baking powder.
    5. Begin to sift the dry ingredients.
    6. Keep the sifted dry ingredients aside.
    7. Grease a loaf pan measuring 8.5 x 4.5 x 2.5 inches with butter and keep aside. You can also use a round pan of 7 or 7.5 inches diameter. You can line the pan with parchment paper or butter paper and then grease. Also, preheat oven to 170°C (335°F).
  2. Whip Butter:
    1. Take unsalted butter in the stand mixer bowl. The butter should be softened, not hard or melted.
    2. With the paddle attachment attached to the mixer, whip the butter on high speed (9 to 10) until the butter becomes light and fluffy. Do not overdo the beating. While beating, stop at intervals and scrape off the butter from the sides and add it back to the center of the bowl.
    3. After whipping, the butter should be light and fluffy.
    4. Add 1 can of sweetened condensed milk (400 grams) to the whipped butter.
    5. Add 1 teaspoon vanilla extract or ½ teaspoon vanilla essence.
    6. Using the stand mixer at low speed (1), mix everything until well blended. Do not over-mix.
  3. Make Butter Cake Batter:
    1. Add the sifted dry ingredients.
    2. With a spatula, lightly mix everything so that the flour particles do not fly when you start the stand mixer.
    3. On low speed (1), mix everything well. Do not over-mix as gluten strands may form, which can ruin the cake structure.
    4. If the batter looks very thick, add about ¼ cup milk or as needed and mix again.
    5. Using a silicone spatula, pour the butter cake batter into the greased loaf pan.
    6. Shake the pan so that the batter moves and settles down at the sides and bottom. Level the batter with a spatula.
  4. Bake Butter Cake:
    1. Bake at 170°C (335°F) in a convection oven (convection ovens have a fan). In an oven without a fan, bake at 180°C (355°F). Keep the pan in the center rack with the bottom heating element on.
    2. Since condensed milk is added, the cake browns faster from the edges and top. Cover the top with aluminum foil. Lightly cover and do not press it on the pan.
    3. Bake for 50 to 60 minutes or until the top looks golden. Check with a toothpick; it should come out clean. Since temperature varies from oven to oven, keep a check and avoid opening the oven door frequently.
    4. Keep the pan on a wire rack to cool.
    5. Once the cake is cooled completely at room temperature, slice it. The butter cake keeps well outside if the temperature is very cold. In warmer climates, keep it for 1 to 2 days at room temperature, then refrigerate.
    6. After refrigerating, the butter will solidify in the cake. Warm the cake in an oven or keep at room temperature before serving.
  5. Serve:
    1. Serve the Butter Cake plain or sprinkle powdered sugar or icing sugar on top. Use a tea strainer to sift the sugar over the cake.
    2. Enjoy as an evening sweet snack with milk, tea, or coffee.

Recipe Tips

Butter Consistency: Ensure the butter is softened but not melted for the best texture. It should be creamy and easy to whip. Sifting: Sifting dry ingredients helps prevent lumps and ensures even distribution of baking powder. Mixing Tips: Avoid over-mixing the batter once the dry ingredients are added. This helps maintain a tender crumb. Pan Preparation: Greasing the pan and optionally lining it with parchment paper ensures easy removal of the cake. Baking Adjustments: Baking times and temperatures can vary based on your oven. Check doneness a few minutes before the end of the suggested baking time.
