Jalebi

Prep Time

15 minutes

Cook Time

20 minutes

Total Time

35 minutes

Servings

4 people

Ingredients

    3. Fry Jalebi: Heat ghee or oil. Test temperature with a small amount of batter. Squeeze batter into concentric rings and fry until golden.
    4. Soak in Syrup: Soak hot fried jalebis in warm sugar syrup for 2 to 3 minutes. Remove and drain excess syrup.

Steps to Make Recipe

Make Batter

  • All-Purpose Flour: 1 cup (125 grams)
  • Besan (Gram Flour): 2 tablespoons
  • Baking Soda: A pinch
  • Turmeric Powder: ⅛ teaspoon (for natural coloring)
  • Water: ¾ to 1 cup (adjust as needed for consistency)

Make Sugar Syrup

  • Sugar: 1 cup
  • Saffron Strands: ¼ teaspoon (or ½ teaspoon cardamom powder and ⅛ teaspoon turmeric powder if saffron is unavailable)
  • Water: ½ cup
  • Lime or Lemon Juice: ¼ teaspoon

Fry Jalebi

  • Ghee or Oil: For deep frying (use ghee for better flavor, or a high smoke point neutral oil like sunflower or vegetable oil)
  1. Make Batter:
    1. In a mixing bowl, take 1 cup (125 grams) all-purpose flour. Add 2 tablespoons besan or gram flour, a pinch of baking soda, and ⅛ teaspoon turmeric powder.
    2. Mix all the dry ingredients very well with a spoon or spatula.
    3. Add ¾ to 1 cup water. The amount of water depends on the flour quality. Aim for a medium consistency and flowing batter.
    4. Mix well with a spatula, spoon, or whisk until the batter is smooth and lump-free. Stir in round circular motions for 4 to 5 minutes to add volume.
    5. Cover and let the batter ferment in a warm place for 12 to 24 hours. Avoid over-fermenting as it will turn sour.
    6. The next day, the batter should have small bubbles on top. Stir and add 1 to 2 tablespoons of all-purpose flour to thicken if necessary.
    7. Mix well and pour the batter into a squeezy bottle or piping bag.
  2. Make Sugar Syrup:
    1. In a pan, take 1 cup sugar and add ¼ teaspoon saffron strands. If saffron is unavailable, use ½ teaspoon cardamom powder and ⅛ teaspoon turmeric powder for color and flavor.
    2. Add ½ cup water and heat over low heat, stirring until the sugar dissolves.
    3. Cook until the syrup reaches one-string consistency. To test, touch a small amount of syrup between your fingers; it should form a single strand.
    4. Once the syrup reaches the right consistency, turn off the heat and add ¼ teaspoon lime or lemon juice. Stir well. Keep the syrup warm on the burner.
  3. Fry Jalebi:
    1. Heat ghee or oil for deep frying in a heavy-bottomed pan. Ghee gives better flavor, but you can use either or a mix of both.
    2. To check the oil temperature, drop a small amount of batter into the oil. It should rise quickly. The proper frying temperature is 350°F (176°C).
    3. Squeeze the batter into concentric rings in the hot oil. Start from the center and work outwards, or vice versa.
    4. Fry until golden brown. Some jalebis will cook faster than others.
    5. Remove with tongs or a bamboo skewer and drain excess oil.
  4. Soak Jalebi In Sugar Syrup:
    1. Immediately place the fried jalebis into the warm sugar syrup.
    2. Turn them over after a minute to coat both sides with syrup.
    3. Soak for 2 to 3 minutes. Shorter soaking time results in a lighter color, while longer soaking gives a deeper color.
    4. Remove from syrup with a skewer or tongs, shake off excess syrup, and place on a plate lined with foil or butter paper.
    5. Repeat with remaining jalebi. It's helpful to have assistance for this process.
    6. Serve jalebi hot, warm, or at room temperature. Leftovers can be stored in an airtight container and refrigerated for up to 5 days.

Recipe Tips

Frying Tips: Fry jalebis in small batches to avoid overcrowding the pan, which can cause uneven cooking. Practice your technique for making concentric rings, as the shapes may vary initially. Syrup Warmth: Keep the syrup warm but not boiling when adding jalebis. Cold syrup can cause jalebis to become soggy instead of absorbing the syrup evenly. Storing Leftovers: Store leftover jalebis in an airtight container to prevent them from becoming too hard. Reheat gently before serving if needed. Serving Suggestions: Jalebi is best enjoyed fresh and warm but can be served at room temperature. It pairs well with a side of milk or rabdi for added flavor.
