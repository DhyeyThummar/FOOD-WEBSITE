Rasgulla

Prep Time

15 minutes

Cook Time

20 minutes

Total Time

35 minutes

Servings

4 people

Ingredients

    Hyderabadi Veg Biryani Ingredients
    • Making Chenna:
      • Whole Milk: 1 litre (about 4 cups)
      • Lemon Juice: 1 to 3 tablespoons (start with 1 tablespoon and add more if needed)
        • Alternative: Vinegar (same amount as lemon juice)
        • Alternative: Curd/Yogurt (4 to 5 tablespoons or more if needed)
    • Making Rasgulla Balls:
      • Unroasted Sooji (Rava/Semolina): 1 teaspoon
        • Alternative: All-purpose Flour (maida) or Cornstarch (for gluten-free option, use 1 teaspoon)
    • Preparing Sugar Syrup:
      • Sugar: 2 cups
      • Water: 4 cups (1 litre)
      • Milk: 1 tablespoon (to remove impurities, optional)
      • Rosewater: 1 to 2 tablespoons (optional, for flavor)
        • Alternative: Kewra Water (pandanus water) or ½ teaspoon cardamom powder (or both)
    • Cooking Rasgulla:
      • Reserved Sugar Solution: ½ cup (set aside from the syrup for adjusting during cooking)

Steps to Make Recipe

  1. Making Chenna
    1. Take 1 litre of whole milk in a pan and bring it to a low boil on a low to medium heat.
      NOTE: If you use buffalo milk, remove the thick layer of malai/cream before proceeding.
    2. While the milk is heating, line a mesh strainer or colander with cheese cloth or muslin.
    3. Stir the milk at intervals to prevent froth and scorching.
    4. When the milk boils, reduce the heat and add 1 to 3 tablespoons of lemon juice. Add more if needed.
      NOTE: You may need extra acid depending on the milk quality. Vinegar or curd/yogurt can be used as alternatives.
    5. Once the milk curdles completely and you see green watery whey, switch off the heat.
    6. Pour the curdled milk into the cheese cloth/muslin-lined strainer.
    7. Rinse the chenna in running water to cool it down and remove the lemony taste.
      NOTE: If using yogurt, rinsing is not necessary.
    8. Squeeze the muslin to drain excess water. Place a heavy weight on the chenna for 7 to 8 minutes or hang it for about 30 minutes.
    9. Remove the cheesecloth. The chenna should be neither too moist nor too dry.
  2. Making Rasgulla Balls
    1. Add 1 teaspoon unroasted sooji (rava/semolina) to the chenna.
      NOTE: You can substitute with all-purpose flour or cornstarch for a gluten-free option.
    2. Mix the sooji with chenna and knead well. Add more moisture if needed by sprinkling water or more flour if too watery.
    3. Knead the chenna until smooth and slightly greasy. Form into smooth balls.
    4. Cover the balls with a moist muslin or kitchen towel and set aside.
  3. Preparing Sugar Syrup
    1. In a large pot, combine 2 cups sugar with 4 cups water and heat until sugar dissolves.
      NOTE: Use a large pot to allow space for the rasgulla to cook.
    2. Add 1 tablespoon of milk to remove impurities. If there are no impurities, skip this step.
    3. Once impurities float to the top, remove them with a spoon or strain through cheese cloth/muslin.
    4. Reserve ½ cup of the sugar solution for later and another 1 cup in a serving bowl.
    5. Bring the remaining sugar solution to a boil.
  4. Cooking Rasgulla
    1. Gently slide the rasgulla balls into the boiling sugar solution.
    2. Shake the pot gently (don’t stir) and cover with a lid. Cook on medium heat.
    3. After 4 minutes, add ¼ cup of reserved sugar solution and shake the pot. Continue to cook.
    4. After another 4 minutes, add another ¼ cup of reserved sugar solution. Cover and cook for 2 more minutes. Total cooking time is about 10 minutes.
    5. To check doneness, place a rasgulla in water; if it sinks, it’s done. Alternatively, press it; if it bounces back, it’s done.
  5. Soaking Rasgulla in Sugar Syrup
    1. Transfer cooked rasgulla to the reserved sugar solution in the serving bowl. Cover and set aside.
    2. Let the syrup cool to warm temperature, then add the remaining sugar solution to the bowl.
    3. Once cooled, add 1 to 2 tablespoons of rosewater (or kewra water/cardamom powder) and stir gently. Soak for about 30 minutes to infuse flavors.
  6. Storage and Serving Suggestions
    1. Serve immediately or refrigerate for later. Keep covered in an airtight container. Rasgulla stays fresh for about a week in the refrigerator.
    2. To serve, place rasgulla in bowls with a few tablespoons of syrup. Garnish with slivered nuts if desired.

Recipe Tips

• Heavy Weight: For draining the chenna • Cheese Cloth/Muslin: For draining chenna • Butter Paper: Optional, for working with the dough • Nuts for Garnish: Slivered almonds or pistachios (optional)
