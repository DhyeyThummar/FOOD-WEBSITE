Besan Laddoo

Prep Time

15 minutes

Cook Time

20 minutes

Total Time

35 minutes

Servings

4 people

Ingredients

    Hyderabadi Veg Biryani Ingredients
    1. Roast Besan
      • Besan (Gram Flour): 2 cups (200 grams)
    2. Roast Ghee With Besan
      • Ghee: ½ cup (125 grams)
    3. Make Laddu Mixture
      • Powdered Sugar or Castor Sugar: 1 cup (175 grams)
        • Alternatively, you can use boora (powdered unrefined cane sugar).
      • Raisins: 1 to 2 tablespoons (chopped)
      • Green Cardamom Powder: 1 teaspoon
      • Cashews: 10 to 12 (finely chopped)
    4. Optional for Shaping
      • Additional Ghee: 2 to 3 tablespoons (hot melted) if the mixture is too dry.

Steps to Make Recipe

  1. Roast Besan
    1. Take a heavy kadai or wok or pan and keep on a low heat. Add 2 cups besan (200 grams) in the kadai. Use besan or gram flour which is within its shelf-period. Make sure it does not taste bitter or has become rancid.
    2. Begin to dry roast besan on low to medium-low heat.
    3. Stir often so that besan gets browned evenly and does not burn. When you begin to roast besan, you will see small lumps in the flour. These lumps disappear later.
    4. Roast until the besan turns golden brown and you can see the color change compared to the beginning.
  2. Roast Ghee With Besan
    1. Add ½ cup ghee (125 grams) to the roasted besan. Preferably use the best quality and fragrant desi ghee – Homemade Ghee is always better.
    2. Mix thoroughly.
    3. Continue to stir often and cook the mixture of besan and ghee for about 12 to 15 minutes. The timing will vary depending on the thickness & size of the pan and intensity of flame.
    4. The mixture will thicken, the color will change, and it will start releasing ghee with a nice fragrant aroma. The ghee will float on top, and the mixture will become one, which indicates that it is fully cooked.
  3. Make Laddu Mixture
    1. Remove the kadai or pan from the stove and place it on the kitchen countertop. Add 1 cup powdered sugar or castor sugar or boora (175 grams) and stir well to avoid lumps.
    2. Mix vigorously to ensure the powdered sugar melts evenly into the flour and ghee mixture.
    3. Add 1 to 2 tablespoons chopped raisins, 1 teaspoon green cardamom powder, and 10 to 12 finely chopped cashews (or other nuts like almonds, pistachios, etc.).
    4. Mix well and let the ladoo mixture become lukewarm or cool at room temperature.
  4. Make Besan Laddu
    1. Shape the mixture into medium-sized laddus with your palms. If you cannot shape them into balls, freeze the mixture for 5 minutes or refrigerate for 20 minutes.
    2. If the mixture looks too dry, add 2 to 3 tablespoons of hot melted ghee and mix again. Let the mixture cool, then shape into laddus.
    3. Store besan ladoo in an airtight steel container.
    4. Serve besan ladoo. You can garnish with some chopped nuts or cashews if desired.

Recipe Tips

Roast Besan: Ensure besan is fresh and not rancid to avoid bitter taste. Roasting should be done on low to medium-low heat to avoid burning. Stir frequently to achieve even browning and to prevent lumps. Roast Ghee With Besan: Use high-quality desi ghee for best flavor; homemade ghee is preferred. The mixture should release ghee and thicken significantly; this indicates that besan is properly roasted. Look for a molten lava consistency and a clear separation of ghee from the mixture.
