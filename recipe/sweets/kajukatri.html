Kaju Katri

Prep Time

15 minutes

Cook Time

20 minutes

Total Time

35 minutes

Servings

4 people

Ingredients

    Hyderabadi Veg Biryani Ingredients
    • Cashews (Kaju): 1 cup
    • Sugar: ½ cup
    • Water: 5 tablespoons
    • Rose Petals (or Rose Water or Saffron):
      • 1 teaspoon rose petals
      • OR 1 teaspoon rose water
      • OR 8 to 9 strands of saffron
    • Ghee (Clarified Butter) or Coconut Oil: 1 tablespoon
    • Optional:
      • Dried Milk Powder: 1 teaspoon (if the dough is too soft)
      • Milk: 1 teaspoon (if the dough is too dry)

    Note: The use of rose petals, rose water, or saffron is optional and based on your preference. The ghee or coconut oil adds a rich flavor and helps in kneading the dough, but can be omitted if desired.

    Steps Summary
    1. Powder Cashews: Grind cashews to a smooth powder, avoiding turning them into a paste.
    2. Make Cashew Dough:
      • Dissolve sugar in water over low heat.
      • Add cashew powder to the sugar syrup and stir continuously until the mixture forms a soft dough.
    3. Knead the Dough:
      • Add rose petals, rose water, or saffron, and ghee or coconut oil.
      • Knead the dough until smooth and roll it out while still warm.
    4. Make Kaju Katli:
      • Cut into squares or diamond shapes once cooled.
      • Store in an airtight container or serve immediately.

Steps to Make Recipe

  • Cashews (Kaju): 1 cup
  • Sugar: ½ cup
  • Water: 5 tablespoons
  • Rose Petals (or Rose Water or Saffron):
    • 1 teaspoon rose petals
    • OR 1 teaspoon rose water
    • OR 8 to 9 strands of saffron
  • Ghee (Clarified Butter) or Coconut Oil: 1 tablespoon
  • Optional:
    • Dried Milk Powder: 1 teaspoon (if the dough is too soft)
    • Milk: 1 teaspoon (if the dough is too dry)
Recipe Steps
  1. Powder Cashews:
    1. Place 1 cup cashews (kaju) in a dry grinder, coffee grinder, or blender. Ensure the cashews are at room temperature and dry. Avoid using refrigerated or frozen cashews as they may release moisture and turn into a paste.
    2. Grind the cashews to a smooth powder, being careful not to over-grind and release the fat. Grind in intervals if needed. The cashews should be powdery, not pasty or buttery. If there are tiny pieces, you can sieve the powder through a medium-fine sieve and set aside.
  2. Make Cashew Dough:
    1. Combine ½ cup sugar and 5 tablespoons water in a thick-bottomed pan or non-stick pan.
    2. Heat on low flame until the sugar dissolves. There is no need to check for syrup consistency; just ensure the sugar is fully dissolved.
    3. Once the sugar is dissolved, add the cashew powder.
    4. Stir continuously on a low flame. Break any lumps as you stir to ensure a smooth dough. This consistent stirring is crucial for a smooth and even dough.
    5. The mixture will thicken and form a soft dough. When this happens, transfer the mixture to a work surface. The dough should form a smooth ball without sticking to your fingers.
  3. Knead the Dough:
    1. Place the cashew dough on a work surface. Add 1 teaspoon rose petals (or 1 teaspoon rose water or 8 to 9 strands of saffron) and 1 tablespoon ghee or coconut oil. Note that the addition of these ingredients is optional.
    2. Allow the dough to cool slightly until it’s just hot enough to handle. Knead lightly, flatten the dough, and place it on a greased plate or tray. Avoid over-kneading as it may release fat from the cashews.
    3. If the dough is too soft, add a teaspoon of dried milk powder. If it is too dry, add a teaspoon of milk to soften it.
    4. You can also place the dough on a large piece of parchment or butter paper. Roll the dough with a rolling pin while still warm. It may have fine cracks, which is normal.
    5. Roll to a thickness of about 3 to 5 mm (about ⅛ inch). Let the rolled dough cool.
  4. Make Kaju Katli:
    1. Once cooled completely, cut the cashew dough into squares or diamond shapes. Trim any uneven edges if desired.
    2. Use a butter knife to gently remove the pieces from the tray or parchment paper. Handle them carefully to avoid breaking.
    3. Arrange the pieces on a plate. Serve immediately or store in an airtight container.

Recipe Tips

Consistency: Dough should be soft but not sticky. If too sticky, cook longer; if too dry, add a bit of milk. Grinding: Use dry cashews and avoid over-grinding to prevent paste formation. Cooking: Use low heat to dissolve sugar and cook the dough to avoid burning. Rolling: Lightly oil the rolling pin or parchment to prevent sticking. Cooling: Allow the dough to cool enough before cutting to avoid misshaping. Storage: Store in an airtight container; it lasts a week at room temperature or longer if refrigerated. Variations: Add cardamom powder for extra flavor. Texture Fix: Use dried milk powder if dough is too soft; add milk if too dry. Serving: Great with chai or coffee, and ideal for special occasions.
