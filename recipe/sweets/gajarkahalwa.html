Gajar Ka Halwa

Prep Time

15 minutes

Cook Time

20 minutes

Total Time

35 minutes

Servings

4 people

Ingredients

    Hyderabadi Veg Biryani Ingredients
    • Prep and Grate Carrots
      • Carrots: 650 grams (about 8 to 9 medium-sized tender, juicy carrots or 6 to 7 long carrots)
    • Cook Carrots
      • Full-Fat Milk: 4 cups
    • Make Gajar Halwa
      • Ghee (Clarified Butter): 4 tablespoons
      • Sugar: 10 to 12 tablespoons (adjust to taste)
      • Cardamom Powder: ½ to 1 teaspoon (or finely powdered green cardamom pods)
      • Nuts and Dry Fruits
        • Cashews: 12 (chopped)
        • Almonds: 12 (chopped)
        • Golden Raisins: 2 tablespoons
        • Optional: Saffron (if using, add at the same time as the nuts and dry fruits)
    1. Prep and Grate Carrots: Rinse, peel, and grate the carrots. You should have about 4 to 4.5 cups of grated carrots.
    2. Cook Carrots: In a heavy kadai or wok, cook grated carrots with full-fat milk on a low-medium heat until the liquid reduces by 75%.
    3. Make Gajar Halwa: Add ghee, sugar, and cardamom powder. Mix well and continue simmering until the mixture thickens. Add chopped nuts, dry fruits, and saffron if using. Cook until the mixture is dry and the milk is completely evaporated.

Steps to Make Recipe

  • Prep and Grate Carrots
    • Carrots: 650 grams (about 8 to 9 medium-sized tender, juicy carrots or 6 to 7 long carrots)
  • Cook Carrots
    • Full-Fat Milk: 4 cups
  • Make Gajar Halwa
    • Ghee (Clarified Butter): 4 tablespoons
    • Sugar: 10 to 12 tablespoons (adjust to taste)
    • Cardamom Powder: ½ to 1 teaspoon (or finely powdered green cardamom pods)
    • Nuts and Dry Fruits
      • Cashews: 12 (chopped)
      • Almonds: 12 (chopped)
      • Golden Raisins: 2 tablespoons
      • Optional: Saffron (if using, add at the same time as the nuts and dry fruits)
  1. Prep and Grate Carrots
    1. First rinse 650 grams carrots (8 to 9 medium-sized tender, juicy carrots or 6 to 7 long carrots) a few times in water. Drain the water. Peel the carrots using a vegetable peeler. Set the peeled carrots aside. Ensure that the carrots are juicy and tender. You could use any variety of carrots (red or orange), but they should be tender.
    2. Next, grate the carrots with a box grater or in a food processor. You can measure the grated carrots with a standard measuring cup and you will need about 4 to 4.5 cups of grated carrots. Grating carrots in a food processor is much easier than grating with a box grater or a hand-held grater.
  2. Cook Carrots
    1. In a heavy kadai or wok, add all of the grated carrots.
    2. Pour in 4 cups full-fat milk. Switch on the burner.
    3. Mix the grated carrots and milk together.
    4. Cook this mixture on a low-medium heat. After a few minutes, the milk will begin to froth and then start slowly reducing.
    5. Keep stirring this halwa mixture often, scraping the sides of the kadai to remove the evaporated milk solids. Stir the milk solids into the cooking mixture.
    6. Cook the carrot and milk mixture until the liquid is reduced by 75%.
  3. Make Gajar Halwa
    1. Add 4 tablespoons ghee (clarified butter). Note that some milk will be there when you add the ghee.
    2. Mix very well.
    3. Next, add 10 to 12 tablespoons sugar, or to taste.
    4. Add cardamom powder – 5 to 6 green cardamom pods powdered finely in a mortar-pestle, or about ½ to 1 teaspoon pre-ground cardamom powder.
    5. Mix very well.
    6. Continue to simmer on a low flame, stirring often.
    7. Simmer till the gajar ka halwa mixture starts to thicken and reduces more. Stir at intervals.
    8. When the halwa has reduced to a pudding-like consistency, add nuts and dry fruits of your choice. I have added 12 cashews, 12 almonds, and 2 tablespoons golden raisins. Note: Chop the nuts before you add. Raisins can be kept whole. If using saffron, then you can add at this step.
    9. Mix again and continue to stir and simmer.
    10. Simmer till the halwa mixture becomes dry. The milk should evaporate completely and you will see fine milk solids in the mixture. You will also see some ghee being released from the sides. Remember to scrape the milk solids stuck at the sides of the kadai or pan and add them back to the carrot halwa. Some moisture is also fine in the halwa.
    11. Serve gajar ka halwa warm from the pan. Refrigerate any leftovers, warming them before serving. Garnish with some chopped dry fruits while serving.

Recipe Tips

arrot Quality: Use tender, juicy carrots for the best flavor and texture. Avoid using dry or overly fibrous carrots as they may affect the taste and texture of the halwa. Milk Reduction: The key to a rich Gajar Halwa is cooking the mixture until the milk is reduced by at least 75%. This process helps concentrate the flavors and achieve the desired consistency. Consistency Check: The halwa should have a pudding-like consistency when done. It should be thick and slightly dry, with no excess liquid. If it’s too liquid, continue simmering until the excess moisture evaporates. Adjusting Sweetness: Sugar quantity can be adjusted based on your taste preference and the sweetness of the carrots. Start with a smaller amount and add more if needed.
