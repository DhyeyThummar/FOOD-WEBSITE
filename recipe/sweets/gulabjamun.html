Gulab Jamun

Prep Time

15 minutes

Cook Time

20 minutes

Total Time

35 minutes

Servings

4 people

Ingredients

    gulabjamun

    • Khoya (Mawa): 1 cup (200 grams)
    • Paneer: ¾ cup (100 grams), grated
    • Fine Rava (Semolina): 2 tablespoons
    • All-Purpose Flour: 2 tablespoons
    • Baking Powder: ¼ teaspoon
    • Cardamom Powder: ½ teaspoon
    • Milk: 1 tablespoon (plus a few teaspoons if needed to form the dough)

    2. Make Sugar Syrup

    • Sugar: 1.75 cups (250 grams)
    • Water: 1 cup
    • Rose Water: 1 to 2 tablespoons

    3. Shape and Fry

    • Oil: For frying (amount depends on the size of the pan; enough to submerge the dough balls)

    4. Optional Garnishes

    • Rose Petals: For garnish (optional)
    • Almond Slivers: For garnish (optional)

    2. Make Sugar Syrup: Dissolve sugar in water, heat until thick and sticky, add rose water, and set aside.
    3. Shape and Fry: Form dough into balls, fry in medium-hot oil, ensuring they are evenly browned. Drain on paper towels.

Steps to Make Recipe

1. Make Jamun Dough

  • Khoya (Mawa): 1 cup (200 grams)
  • Paneer: ¾ cup (100 grams), grated
  • Fine Rava (Semolina): 2 tablespoons
  • All-Purpose Flour: 2 tablespoons
  • Baking Powder: ¼ teaspoon
  • Cardamom Powder: ½ teaspoon
  • Milk: 1 tablespoon (plus a few teaspoons if needed to form the dough)

2. Make Sugar Syrup

  • Sugar: 1.75 cups (250 grams)
  • Water: 1 cup
  • Rose Water: 1 to 2 tablespoons

3. Shape and Fry

  • Oil: For frying (amount depends on the size of the pan; enough to submerge the dough balls)

4. Optional Garnishes

  • Rose Petals: For garnish (optional)
  • Almond Slivers: For garnish (optional)
  1. Make Jamun Dough:
    1. Take 1 cup khoya or mawa (200 grams) in a bowl. Soft khoya, also known as ‘daap ka khoya’ or ‘chikna khoya,’ is used. This soft khoya mashes and kneads very well.
    2. Mash khoya very well, ensuring there are no lumps or small bits lurking.
    3. Add ¾ cup (100 grams) grated paneer, 2 tablespoons fine rava (semolina), 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour, ¼ teaspoon baking powder, and ½ teaspoon cardamom powder to the mashed khoya. Ensure there are no lumps in either the mawa or paneer.
    4. Mix well.
    5. Add 1 tablespoon milk and gather together to form a dough. Don’t knead, just gently mix. If the mixture appears dry, add a few teaspoons of milk and mix again. Cover the dough and set aside for 30 minutes.
  2. Make Sugar Syrup:
    1. Mix 1.75 cups (250 grams) sugar in 1 cup water.
    2. On a medium-low flame, heat the sugar solution until it becomes thick and sticky. Switch off the heat before the syrup reaches a one-thread consistency. Raw sugar contributes to the dark color of the syrup.
    3. Add rose water and stir. Set the sugar solution aside. If the syrup crystallizes while cooling, add 2 to 3 tablespoons of water and warm the syrup again, stirring while warming.
  3. Shape and Fry:
    1. After 30 minutes, make small balls from the dough without cracks. Cover the dough balls and set aside.
    2. Heat oil until it is medium-hot. Lower the heat to medium-low or low and wait for a minute. Gently place a single dough ball in the oil. If the dough ball breaks while frying, add some more maida (about 1-2 tablespoons) to the dough. Mix gently again and let the dough sit for fifteen minutes before shaping and frying.
    3. Once the jamun starts to develop tiny golden spots, rotate them in the oil to ensure even browning. Remove the fried dough balls and place them on paper towels to remove extra oil.
  4. Soak Jamun in Sugar Syrup:
    1. Place the hot fried dough balls in the sugar syrup. Continue to fry the rest of the dough balls in batches, draining them quickly on paper towels before adding them to the syrup while still hot.
    2. Once all the gulab jamun are in the sugar syrup, keep the pan on a low flame for 1 to 2 minutes. Heating helps the jamun absorb the syrup and become soft. The gulab jamun will increase slightly in size during soaking. Don’t overcook them to avoid breaking.
    3. Use a large enough pan so that the fried jamun balls are not overcrowded and can be stirred gently while simmering.
    4. Serve gulab jamun warm or at room temperature. You can also chill them and serve cold. Garnish with rose petals or almond slivers if desired. Enjoy!

Recipe Tips

Khoya Type: Use soft khoya (daap ka khoya or chikna khoya) for a smooth dough. Hard khoya may not yield the same texture. Paneer: Ensure the paneer is finely grated to blend seamlessly with the khoya. If paneer is too coarse, it may affect the smoothness of the dough. Dough Consistency: If the dough is too dry, add milk a little at a time. If it’s too wet, incorporate a bit more flour. Frying Temperature: Maintain medium-hot oil to prevent the jamuns from browning too quickly. If oil is too hot, the jamuns may cook unevenly or burn. Oil Quality: Use a neutral oil like vegetable or canola oil for frying, as it won't impart any strong flavor to the jamuns.
