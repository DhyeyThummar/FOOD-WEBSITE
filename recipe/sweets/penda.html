Steps to Make Recipe

Peda Recipe

Make Khoya Mixture

  • Khoya (Mawa): 200 to 205 grams (about 1 cup, tightly packed, grated)
  • Sugar: ½ cup (125 grams)
  • Cardamom Powder: ½ teaspoon

Cook Peda Mixture

  • Milk: 2 to 3 tablespoons

Garnish

  • Slivered Pistachios: For topping
  • Slivered Almonds: For topping (optional)
  1. Make Khoya Mixture:
    1. Grate 200 to 205 grams (about 7.05 oz) of homemade or store-bought khoya. You will need 1 cup tightly packed grated khoya. Add this to a deep pan or kadai. Use a heavy and thick-bottomed pan so that the khoya mixture does not brown or burn at the bottom.
    2. Add ½ cup sugar (125 grams) and ½ teaspoon cardamom powder.
    3. Set the heat to low. Mix in the khoya with the sugar.
    4. Mix evenly and very well.
    5. Add 2 to 3 tablespoons milk.
  2. Cook Peda Mixture:
    1. Mix well. You will see the sugar melting and the mixture becomes more liquid.
    2. Simmer the mixture on low heat while stirring continuously.
    3. Stir non-stop to ensure the mixture does not get browned or stick to the bottom of the pan using a wooden spatula.
    4. As you stir, the mixture will start to bubble.
    5. Continue to stir and cook until the mixture thickens and starts to pull away from the side of the pan.
  3. Cool Doodh Peda Mixture:
    1. Immediately pour the entire molten peda mixture onto a steel plate or tray. It should be thick but still spread a bit. Avoid overcooking the peda to prevent it from becoming chewy.
    2. Let the mixture cool to room temperature.
  4. Shape Peda:
    1. Take small portions from the lukewarm peda mixture and roll them into small-to-medium balls, about the size of a small to medium lime.
    2. Place a few slivered pistachios or almonds on top of each milk peda ball.
    3. With a peda maker or peda press, gently press each ball to flatten and shape the milk fudge. If you don’t have a peda maker, use a flat-bottomed bowl or a small steel glass to press them into a thick disc shape. Alternatively, roll the mixture into balls and then flatten them with your palms, pressing a few almonds or pistachios on top.
    4. Top with additional nuts if desired.
    5. Enjoy right away or store the milk peda at room temperature for a few hours before serving. Refrigerate leftovers in an airtight container for about a week. Peda can be eaten at room temperature or cold.
    6. You can also make a large batch and gift them for Diwali or any Indian festival in decorative boxes.

Recipe Tips

Khoya: Ensure the khoya (mawa) is fresh and soft for the best texture and taste. If using store-bought khoya, grate it finely before using. Sugar Adjustments: You can adjust the sugar amount based on your sweetness preference, but remember that reducing sugar too much can affect the texture. Consistency Check: The mixture should be thick but still slightly pliable when ready. If it becomes too dry, add a bit more milk to adjust the consistency. Cooling Tips: To speed up cooling, you can spread the mixture on a wider surface. Ensure it cools to room temperature before shaping to avoid sticky hands.
