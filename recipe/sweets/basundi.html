Basundi

Prep Time

15 minutes

Cook Time

20 minutes

Total Time

35 minutes

Servings

4 people

Ingredients

    Hyderabadi Veg Biryani Ingredients
    • Prep and Cook Basundi Mixture
      • Condensed Milk: ½ can (200 grams) or ½ cup + 2 tablespoons
      • Whole Milk: 2 cups (adjust as needed)
    • Simmer Mixture
      • Nuts:
        • Cashews: 6, slivered
        • Pistachios: 10, slivered
        • Almonds: 8, slivered
      • Nutmeg: A pinch of grated nutmeg or ground nutmeg powder
      • Cardamom Powder: ½ teaspoon (or 4 green cardamom pods, powdered)
      • Saffron Strands: 10 to 12 strands
      • Chironji (Charoli Seeds): 2 tablespoons

Steps to Make Recipe

  1. Prep and Cook Basundi Mixture
    1. Firstly, take ½ a can of condensed milk (200 grams) or ½ cup + 2 tablespoons in a heavy skillet or thick-bottomed saucepan or kadai (wok).
    2. Add 2 cups of whole milk. Do not use low-fat or skimmed milk. Note: How much milk is needed depends on how much sugar is in the sweetened condensed milk you use. Start with 2 cups of milk, and add more as needed after the sweetened condensed milk has been added.
    3. With a whisk, mix well enough to combine the thick condensed milk with whole milk to get an even liquid. The mixture should be even, or else the condensed milk at the bottom can burn while cooking. So make sure there is no condensed milk at the bottom of the pan. All the condensed milk should be mixed with the milk.
    4. Switch on the heat and keep it on low.
  2. Simmer Mixture
    1. Place the pan on the stove-top and simmer for 20 to 25 minutes. Stir after intervals of 3 to 4 minutes on low heat. Continue to stir often as you bring the mixture to a gentle boil. Be careful not to let the milk brown on the bottom of the pot.
    2. As it cooks, use a rubber spatula or wooden spatula to scrape the sides of the pan and stir the milk solids back into the basundi mixture.
    3. Meanwhile, chop the nuts and set them aside. Add your choice of chopped nuts. My favorite nuts to include are: cashews, pistachios, almonds. Here I have slivered 6 cashews, 10 pistachios, and 8 almonds. You can add more or less of each of the nuts.
    4. The basundi mixture will begin to thicken as you continue to cook it. After it simmers for 20 to 25 minutes, the basundi should be slightly thick but smooth and creamy.
  3. Make Basundi Sweet
    1. Add the slivered nuts.
    2. Grate nutmeg to get a pinch of it or add a generous pinch of ground nutmeg powder.
    3. Add ½ teaspoon cardamom powder (or 4 green cardamom pods, powdered in a mortar-pestle) and 10 to 12 strands of saffron. Also add 2 tablespoons of chironji (charoli seeds). Make sure that the chironji is fresh and not rancid or tasting bitter. As they get rancid very quickly. Chironji is also known as charoli, and these small brown seeds give a lovely crunch and nuttiness to this creamy dessert.
    4. Stir to mix and simmer it for another minute. Turn off the heat.
  4. Serving and Storage Suggestions
    1. Transfer the basundi sweet to serving bowl(s). Serve this creamy dessert hot, warm, or chilled, garnished with some almond and pistachio slivers.

Recipe Tips

Spices & Flavorings: Nutmeg, cardamom, and saffron enhance flavor. Ensure saffron is well-distributed by stirring thoroughly. Chironji: Use fresh chironji (charoli seeds) for best flavor and crunch. Check for rancidity before use. Consistency: The basundi should be slightly thick but smooth and creamy. Adjust cooking time if needed to reach desired thickness. Serving: Serve hot, warm, or chilled, garnished with additional nut slivers for presentation.
